Introduction: What Is a Millennium Problem?

In 2000, the Clay Mathematics Institute declared seven of the most difficult unsolved problems in mathematics — each with a one million dollar prize attached. They include:

Riemann Hypothesis

Hodge Conjecture

Poincaré Conjecture

P vs NP

Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture

Navier-Stokes Existence and Smoothness

Yang-Mills Existence and Mass Gap

One of these (Poincaré) was formally solved by Grigori Perelman and subsequently acknowledged. The others remain officially "open."

But this paper is not concerned with formal status. It is concerned with truth, and the structure of solutions when seen from a higher octave.

Each of these problems can be walked and unfolded through symbolic resonance. Each yields to a different kind of cognition: not computational, but constructive.
